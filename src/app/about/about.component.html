Who am I?

Hey there! I’m a software developer who loves turning messy ideas into clean, scalable code that actually works. Angular and Java are my favorite tools, and I get a real buzz out of building smooth, user-friendly interfaces that make people’s lives easier (and maybe even a little more fun).

I’ve been coding since 2018, but I’ve always been a problem-solver at heart. My background in mechanical engineering taught me how to think systematically, break things down, find the logic, and build them back up smarter than before. That mindset naturally carried over into software development.

I started my professional journey at Union Systems Limited, Africa’s leading financial software company. Union Systems delivers forward-thinking digital solutions that help banks and corporates across the continent achieve full automation and digitisation of their trade operations.

As a frontend developer, I worked on a B2B trade finance platform, building complex UI components such as import documents, letters of credit, and warrants forms using Angular, HTML, CSS, and TypeScript. When the backend team hit a gap, I decided to dive into Java with spring boot and MySQL to help out. Before long, I was developing backend logic and API endpoints, bridging the gap between frontend and backend to keep the whole system moving. That experience turned me into a full-stack developer and gave me a deeper understanding of how the entire product comes together.

While at Union Systems, I also took on a contract role with Woodcore Inc., a company that empowers commercial banks, microfinance banks, neobanks, fintechs, and credit unions with robust banking technology. Woodcore’s comprehensive solutions help financial institutions deliver superior products and services that truly delight their customers.

At Woodcore, I worked as a frontend developer on several projects, including two core banking applications: one for banks and another for customers. I also developed a mobile onboarding app and an internal admin platform that helped teams work more efficiently. My job was to turn Figma designs into responsive, engaging interfaces using Angular, Bootstrap, and a few handy npm packages. Some projects were team efforts that strengthened my collaboration skills, while others I handled independently, which really sharpened my problem-solving abilities and sense of ownership.

After a few years in the field, I realised I wanted to grow beyond the code to understand the bigger picture of technology, people, and design. I saw that I could be even more valuable if I deepened my knowledge and broadened my perspective. That realisation led me on a personal quest for growth, where I pursued a Master’s in Information Technology at Bournemouth University (UK).

There, I explored human-centred design, cloud computing, data management, and persuasive technology; subjects that connect code to real human impact. Along the way, I freelanced, experimented, and stayed hands-on with the latest tools and trends, making sure the learning never stopped.

Now, I’m ready to jump back into a team where I can build things that matter, keep learning on the fly, and have some fun doing it. When I’m not buried in code, you’ll probably find me watching football highlights, binging a great movie, or going down a rabbit hole with some new dev tool I just discovered. Always learning, always curious, always building.
